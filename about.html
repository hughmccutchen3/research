About

Hugh McCutchen

Twenty years leading complex work where technology meets the business, now applied to research and writing with large language models.

Where I work

I work where technology meets the business. The organization relies on me when a system has to be designed across technical, organizational, and political dimensions at once, and then built across people and teams I do not have direct authority over. The hard part is rarely the technology. It is getting a complex thing into production, keeping it there, and holding the trust of everyone who touches it.

That is the same work an LLM asks for when you use it seriously. You supply the vision and the grounding it cannot supply for itself, you direct it through the parts, and you decide when the work has earned confidence. Bursera is that practice offered as a service: research, analysis, and writing run with an LLM framework, and training for the leaders who want to run the framework themselves.

How I got here

I started as an architectural designer. Systems-first thinking came from there — read the whole structure before the details, design for how the parts carry load together — and it moved with me when I pivoted into technology. For a thirty-person investor relations firm I became the sole owner of the full technology stack and led a complete application rebuild from scratch, which is where the architecture habit met real software.

The next twenty years were healthcare data at McKesson and CoverMyMeds: petabyte-scale platforms, governance built in from the start, and the cross-organization trust it takes to keep complex systems running. The tools changed the whole way. The work did not. Every version of it has been the same thing — leading a complex build through people, toward a decision someone can act on.

Track record

  • Platform
    Led data platform strategy for a 200-plus engineer organization and a petabyte of data across five legal entities, and originated the five-zone Databricks platform that became the company's enterprise standard.
  • Governance
    Authored the controls framework and SOX-readiness package that privacy leadership called the first to address analytical data risk end to end, and set the policy that let more than a dozen teams develop against governed production data.

  • Adoption
    Founded an AI adoption program that reached twenty-plus members and ten-plus documented use cases in its first two weeks, with early adopters reaching five-to-ten-times task speed inside the first month.
